Editing

Editing is a skill that I have developed over time through a combination of formal education, self-study, and practical experience. My journey with editing began when I first picked up a camera and started taking photographs. I quickly realized that taking a good photo was not enough; it needed to be edited in order to truly capture the emotion and story I wanted to convey.

I continued to practice and learn on my own, experimenting with different editing styles and techniques. I would often watch editing tutorials and read articles online, to learn new skills and to stay updated with the latest editing trends. I also joined online photography communities, where I could share my work and receive feedback from other photographers. This helped me to see my work from different perspectives and to improve upon it

As I gained more experience, I found that editing became more than just a hobby, it became a passion. I learned that editing is not just about making things look good, but about creating a mood and evoking emotion. I will continue to improve my skills and evolve my editing style in the future.

Filming

Filming has been a passion of mine ever since I was a teenager. I remember the moment when I first held a camera in my hands, and I knew that filmmaking was something I wanted to pursue. However, I didn't know where to start, and learning how to film was a challenging but rewarding journey.

As a teenager, my first step in learning how to film was watching and studying films. I would watch movies and analyze their camera angles, shots, and movements. I also read books and articles about the craft of filmmaking, learning about different techniques and styles.

Learning how to film as a teenager was a journey that required patience, perseverance, and a willingness to experiment. It taught me valuable lessons about the craft of filmmaking and helped me develop my own style and voice. I look forward to continuing to learn and grow as a filmmaker in the future.

Photography

Learning photography as a teenager with no prior experience and using only a phone as my camera was a challenging but rewarding journey. It taught me the importance of patience, experimentation, and creativity.

As a teenager, my first step in learning photography was to simply start taking pictures with my phone. I would take photos of anything and everything, experimenting with different angles and compositions. I quickly realized that the camera on my phone was limited in terms of settings and options, but I learned to work with what I had and to make the most of it.

I also started experimenting with different subjects and styles of photography. I would take pictures of my friends, nature, and street scenes. I would try different techniques such as long exposures and panoramas. I would then look at the photos, and try to understand what worked and what didn't.

Learning photography as a teenager with no prior experience and using only a phone as my camera was a challenging but rewarding journey. It taught me the importance of patience, experimentation, and creativity and helped me develop my own style and voice as a photographer. I look forward to continuing to learn and grow as a photographer in the future.
